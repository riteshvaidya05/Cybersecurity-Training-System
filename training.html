Top 5 Common Cyber Attacks

1. Phishing Attacks

What is it?: Phishing is a cyberattack where fake emails, websites, or messages trick users into providing personal or login information. These attacks often pretend to be from trusted sources like banks, coworkers, or services you use.

Prevention: Never click on suspicious or unknown links.
Always check the sender's email address carefully.
Use spam filters and email verification tools.
Train employees with simulated phishing exercises.

2. Malware

What is it? Malware is malicious software designed to damage or steal data from devices. It includes viruses, spyware, trojans, and worms, often installed without the user’s knowledge.

Prevention: Keep software and systems fully updated.
Use reliable antivirus and anti-malware software.
Avoid downloading files or apps from unknown sources.
Disable automatic downloads in browsers and emails.

3. Ransomware

What is it? Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts files and demands payment to unlock them. Victims may lose access to important data permanently.

Prevention:Regularly back up important data offline.
Avoid clicking on unknown links or attachments.
Keep all software patched and secure.
Use strong email security tools and endpoint protection.

4. Wi-Fi Network Attacks

What is it?Wi-Fi network attacks happen when hackers intercept unencrypted data on public or weak networks. They can steal passwords, emails, and other private information.

Prevention: Always use a VPN on public Wi-Fi.
Never access sensitive information over public networks.
Disable auto-connect to open Wi-Fi networks.
Ensure your home or office Wi-Fi uses strong encryption (WPA3).

5. Social Engineering

What is it? Social engineering uses manipulation or deception to trick people into revealing confidential information or granting access to systems.

Prevention: Be skeptical of unexpected requests for sensitive info.
Verify identities before sharing any confidential data.
Provide regular training to staff on manipulation tactics.
Establish strict protocols for handling private information.
